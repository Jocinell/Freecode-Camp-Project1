FUMILAYO RANSOM KUTI

ONE OF THE GREATEST REVOLUTIONARY

Here's a time line of olufumilayo ransome kuti life:

  • 1900 - Born as Fracis Abigail olufumilayo Thomas on October 25, 1900 in Abeokuta , in present-day Ogun State, Nigeria
  • 1906-1913 - she attended St John Primary School, igbe in Abeokuta from 1906 to 1913 before becoming the first female student at Abeokuta Grammar School.
  • 1914-1917 - she attended secondary school this period and also taught briefly before leaving to further her education at the Wincham Hall School for Girls in Cheshire,England from 1919 to 1923.
  • 1925 - she resumed at abeokuta on her return drop her english name for the yoruba name Fumilayo and got married to Isreal Oladotun Ransom-kuti,an Anglican clergyman and teacher, when her husband became the principal of the Abeokuta school in 1932, she helped organise the Abeokuta ladies club(ALC).
  • 1944 - The ALC was initially a civic and charitably group of mostly Western-Educated Christian Women.Over time it became more, political and feminist.By 1944 , the organisattion formally admitted market Women, who were exploited by colonial authorities.
  • 1946 - Alc became open to all women in 1946 after changing it name to Abeokuta Women Union (AWU) and making Kuti the first president of AWU.Under her leadership, AWU became National Organisation, renamed itself the Nigeria Women Union NWU IN 1949 AND EVENTUALLY THE FEDERATION of NIGERIA SOCIETIES(FNWS) IN 1953.
  • 1970S in the 1970s after the death of her husband, she along with her youngest son fela , changed their last-name to Anikulapo kuti:Anikulapo in yoruba roughly means "a warrior who carrys powerfu; protection in is pocket" or he who carries death in his pouch".
  • 1978 - Fumilayo Ransom Kuti, played essential roles in edducation Healthcare,political activism and thje arts. Her grandchildern, seun Kuti and Femi Kuti also stand out in their field. she was thrown from a second-floor window in 1978 when a thousand armed military personnel stormed her son Fela"s Compound(known as kalakuta Republic).The fatal injuries she sustained from the incident put her in coma sometimes in feburary before she passed away on April 13th. She was buried in Abeokuta on May 5, 1978.

"fumilayo ransome kuti way of life and achievement are testimony to the far-reaching contribution that one man's towering freedom, rights and vision can make to country peace and progress."
